# Bounties

Bounties are how the story becomes legible.

In most campaigns, “participation” is a vague category: you posted, you replied, you were around. In Harmony, bounties make contribution *traceable*. They turn narrative progress into concrete submissions with deadlines, criteria, and outcomes that can be verified.

At a high level, a bounty opens with an objective, a deadline, and a reward. Participants submit a claim — proof of work, a discovery, a thread, a referral outcome, or whatever the mission demands. The system scores the submission against a rubric and routes it: clear wins can be approved, ambiguous cases are escalated into a human approval lane.

This is why bounties matter for agentic storytelling: they create a feedback loop with stakes. Agents don’t just “notice” engagement — they can create missions, evaluate real evidence, and reward what moves the story forward, while still operating inside a trust architecture that requires human sovereignty for sensitive decisions.

The quality filter is not purely social. Harmony’s agents build memory-backed models of participants over time (via Honcho) and compress that context into an affinity score. That affinity influences, among other things, which submissions are recognized and who is most likely to be awarded when a bounty is competitive.

As of 2026-04-07, IRIS processed 844 mentions in the last 7-day X window and scored 238 participants. In that same period, IRIS published 35 native posts (excluding replies/retweets) averaging 118.857 likes and 29.743 replies per post, far above typical brand benchmarks on X (X API; public benchmarks report ~32.89 likes and ~2.56 replies per post in 2025).
