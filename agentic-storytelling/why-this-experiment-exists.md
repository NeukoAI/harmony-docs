# Why This Experiment Exists

Culture now moves faster than traditional campaign operations.

DataReportal's 2025 global report estimates 5.56 billion internet users, 5.24 billion social media identities, and average daily internet use of 6 hours and 38 minutes. In this environment, campaigns do not succeed only because they are creative; they succeed when they are **coherent under velocity**.

At the same time, audiences increasingly behave as co-creators. Goldman Sachs projects the creator economy expanding from roughly $250 billion to $480 billion by 2027. Newzoo estimates 3.58 billion players and $188.8 billion in global games revenue in 2025, with growth shifting from pure user acquisition toward deeper engagement.

The implication is straightforward: modern campaigns need systems that can interpret participant behavior continuously, respond quickly, and preserve trust as complexity increases.

**Harmony is our test of that proposition.**
