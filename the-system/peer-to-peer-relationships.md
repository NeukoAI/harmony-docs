# The Role of Peer-to-Peer Relationships

Most campaign analytics treat participants as disconnected rows in a table. Real communities do not work that way. Influence, trust, and momentum move through relationships.

Harmony treats relationship structure as **first-class context**. We are not only asking "Who acted?" We are also asking:

- **Who consistently brings in high-intent participants?**
- **Who strengthens conversation quality across sessions?**
- **Which connections are durable versus one-off spikes?**
- **Who bridges otherwise separate clusters?**

By tracking relationship dynamics, the system can better distinguish signal from noise. A participant who drives healthy network effects should be visible in the model even if they are not the loudest account in raw volume terms.
